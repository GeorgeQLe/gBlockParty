### The big picture

Spring 2026 marks an inflection point. Foundation models have matured past the "wow, it can write code" phase into genuine developer infrastructure. The conversation has shifted from _can AI do X?_ to _how do we ship reliably with AI in the loop?_

### What shipped

- **Coding agents went mainstream.** Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf, and Devin are no longer experiments — teams are building real workflows around them.
- **Context windows got practical.** Models now routinely handle 100k+ tokens, making whole-repo reasoning viable for the first time.
- **Open-weight models closed the gap.** Llama, Mistral, and DeepSeek are competitive enough that self-hosting is a real option for startups watching their API bills.

### What stalled

- **Multimodal didn't land like expected.** Vision and audio capabilities exist but haven't found their killer app in dev tooling yet.
- **Agent reliability is still the bottleneck.** Agents can do impressive demos, but production error rates keep teams reaching for the manual override.

### What to watch

- **MCP and tool-use standards** — will the ecosystem converge on a shared protocol for agent ↔ tool communication?
- **Cost curves** — inference pricing is dropping fast enough that use cases that were uneconomical six months ago are now viable.
- **Regulation** — the EU AI Act enforcement timeline is tightening; US executive orders are still evolving.
